Katibawasan Falls is 250 feet high, or around 76 meters. It is the tallest waterfall in Camiguin, and one of the tallest single-drop waterfalls in the Philippines.

Sunken Cemetery is known for its large cross monument in the middle of the sea. It’s a great place to watch the sunset or admire Camiguin’s mountains from offshore.

Tuasan Falls is a beautiful single cascade waterfall with crystal-clear water surrounded by lush jungle.

The Old Church Ruins in Camiguin are all that remain of the Guinsiliban Church, also known as the Old Catarman Church, which was built in the early 16th century.

Soda Water Pool is one of the most popular spring resorts in Camiguin. Its clean and clear water is not just good for swimming but for drinking as well.

Ardent Hot Spring is a popular natural thermal spring that offers a relaxing escape amidst the island’s lush landscapes.

Sto. Niño Cold Spring is best known for its cool and fresh waters that make for a great escape, especially during the summer months of March to May.

Mantigue Island is a serene tropical getaway in Camiguin, offering stunning white sands, clear waters, and vibrant marine life. It’s the perfect escape for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

White Island is known for its tendency to change shape depending on the tide.

Katunggan Park isn’t just a normal park—it’s a web of boardwalks through the mangroves that lead to huts perched over the crystal-clear ocean.

Giant Clams Sanctuary is located on Kibila White Beach, and is home to giant clams which are an endangered species. At this sanctuary, you’ll be able to snorkel, swim, and learn more about these majestic creatures.
